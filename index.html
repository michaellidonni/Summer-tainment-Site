About the Artist

Donald Glover, born in 1983, is an American writer, comedian, actor, and musician known for his TV series Atlanta and music under the name Childish Gambino. Born in Stone Mountain, Georgia, Glover attended DeKalb School of the Arts and New York University's Tisch School of the Arts. He was a founding member of Derrick Comedy and began his rap career. Glover's music career evolved with mixtapes like Sick Boi (2008) and Poindexter (2009), followed by albums like Because the Internet (2013) and "3005". His debut song, "This Is America," won four Grammy Awards.
